Core Ideas

Ten Principles

1. Human Dignity is Fundamental

All our activities center on human dignity. Technology, data, software, or decision-making mechanisms must never supersede humanity.

Quote: “A human is God's vicegerent on earth; anything that harms them betrays the divine trust.” — Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) [1]

2. Ethical Leadership Stems from Responsibility

Decision-making power comes not from authority but from integrity and accountability. Leadership finds meaning in serving the community.

Quote: “Leadership is about offering freedom to others.” — Richard Stallman [2]

3. Pluralism is Strength

Diverse voices and disciplines are not conflict but richness. We believe in a dialogue culture that listens and unites.

Quote: “Differences are God's law. Respecting them is what makes us human.” — Alija Izetbegović [3]

4. Open Knowledge is a Shared Asset

Knowledge is not to be hoarded but shared. Openness means not just access but virtuous production.

Quote: “Knowledge is the foundation of freedom; sharing it grows humanity's legacy.” — Linus Torvalds [4]

5. Consultation with Community Shapes Decisions

Final decisions may rest with the decision-maker, but the process is shaped by the community. We build open, learning, and participatory decision structures.

Quote: “A community's strength lies in listening to every voice.” — Eric S. Raymond [5]

6. Balance of Freedom and Responsibility

Freedom is not recklessness but a reflection of responsibility. We uphold this balance in code and relationships.

Quote: “Freedom is complete only when it respects the freedom of others.” — Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) [6]

7. Digital Justice and Accessibility

Our technological solutions must be accessible to all. Justice begins in the digital realm, fostering inclusion, not discrimination.

Quote: “Accessibility is the conscience of technology.” — Tim Berners-Lee [7]

8. Principles Precede Profit

Profit must never override principles. Ethical production is our definition of sustainability.

Quote: “Morality must be the compass of every decision.” — Alija Izetbegović [8]

9. Transparency Builds Trust

We are transparent in all our processes. Trust is built through openness and honesty.

Quote: “Transparency is the soul of a system; without it, nothing endures.” — Julian Assange [9]

10. Technology Serves Humanity

Technology is a tool for human welfare. Every innovation must benefit society.

Quote: “Technology should liberate humanity, not chain it.” — Aaron Swartz [10]

References

Notes on the declaration

The declaration draws from Islamic sources, free software pioneers, and web advocates. Several statements are paraphrased to fit a concise, inspirational style while staying grounded in primary sources.

These notes preserve the original structure: paraphrasing, Islamic sources, free software pioneers, and references to Izetbegović and Assange.

  • 1. Paraphrased from Islamic teachings on human dignity as God’s vicegerent (e.g., Qur’an 2:30 and hadith in Sahih al-Bukhari). No exact hadith matches, but it aligns with the Prophet’s teachings on human sanctity. Back to reference 1
  • 2. Inspired by Stallman’s Free Software, Free Society (2002), emphasizing leadership through enabling freedom. Back to reference 2
  • 3. Paraphrased from Izetbegović’s Islamic Declaration (1970), reflecting his views on diversity. Back to reference 3
  • 4. Paraphrased from Torvalds’ Just for Fun (2001), emphasizing open-source knowledge sharing. Back to reference 4
  • 5. Derived from Raymond’s The Cathedral and the Bazaar (1999), advocating community-driven development. Back to reference 5
  • 6. Paraphrased from hadith on justice and mutual respect, such as in Sahih Muslim, reflecting freedom and duty. Back to reference 6
  • 7. Inspired by Berners-Lee’s Weaving the Web (1999), advocating for universal web access. Back to reference 7
  • 8. Paraphrased from Izetbegović’s Islam Between East and West (1984), emphasizing moral guidance. Back to reference 8
  • 9. Based on Assange’s Cypherpunks: Freedom and the Future of the Internet (2012), on transparency. Back to reference 9
  • 10. Paraphrased from Swartz’s Guerilla Open Access Manifesto (2008), advocating for liberating technology. Back to reference 10
